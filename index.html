About Our Lab

The TechLing Lab is a cutting-edge research facility at the forefront of computational linguistics and digital humanities. We investigate how technology transforms the way we process, translate, and retell linguistic content.

Our interdisciplinary approach combines computational linguistics, translation studies, and narrative theory to address fundamental questions about language in the digital era.

We develop innovative methodologies for analyzing retranslations, study how stories evolve across digital platforms, and explore the ethical implications of AI in linguistic research.

Our mission: Bridge traditional humanistic inquiry with modern computational methods, creating new possibilities for understanding language, culture, and narrative in our interconnected world.

Research Areas

🔬 Technology-Enhanced Linguistics

Investigating how computational tools transform linguistic analysis, from corpus linguistics to machine learning applications. We develop new methodologies for digital humanities research and explore AI's role in language study.

🔄 Retranslation Studies

Examining the phenomena of retranslation across cultures and time periods. Our research explores why texts are translated multiple times and how each translation reflects its historical and cultural context.

📖 Digital Retellings

Studying how stories adapt and evolve across digital platforms. From social media narratives to interactive storytelling, we analyze the impact of technology on narrative structures and cultural transmission.

🤖 AI and Translation

Exploring the role of artificial intelligence in translation processes. We study human-AI collaboration in translation, quality assessment of machine-generated translations, and ethical considerations.

🌐 Multilingual Digital Humanities

Developing computational methods for analyzing multilingual texts and cultural artifacts. Our work supports digital preservation of linguistic diversity and cross-cultural understanding.

📱 Language Technology Ethics

Investigating the ethical implications of language technologies, including bias in AI systems, digital language rights, and the impact of automation on linguistic communities and cultural heritage.
