About the Project

üî¨

Digital Humanities Innovation

Creating an electronic database with information on argument structure of selected verbs across different periods and language families, combining corpus-driven methods with genealogical and contact linguistics.

üåê

Cross-Linguistic Approach

Focusing on early Greek, Romance, and Germanic dialects to investigate the complementary role of linguistic genealogy and language contact in the history of languages.

üìä

Diachronic Perspective

Adding the temporal dimension to valency studies by collecting evidence from languages with well-documented histories, approaching valency and valency-changing categories across time.

Project Objectives

Main Research Questions

  1. Linguistic Genealogy vs. Contact: How do genealogical characteristics and contact-induced changes interact in valency patterns?
  2. Diachronic Analysis: What are the major diachronic features of valency categories across different language families?
  3. Methodological Innovation: How can we create a unified resource combining dictionaries, corpora, and typological databases?

Work Packages

WP1 - Project Management Months 1-24

Coordination and Administration

Overall project coordination, financial management, quality assurance, and dissemination activities.

WP2 - Text Selection & Analysis Months 1-3

Corpus Design and Sample Selection

Selection of texts from different periods, creation of annotation schemes, cross-linguistic analysis of valency patterns.

  • Ancient Greek texts (Homer to Byzantine)
  • Latin and Romance texts
  • Germanic historical texts
WP3 - Annotation Months 4-15

Valency Features Annotation

Systematic annotation of valency features in selected texts, with emphasis on loanwords (contact) and native words (genealogy).

Person Months: 38 PMs

WP4 - Automatic Extraction Months 12-17

Information Extraction System

Development of codes and formalisms for automatic extraction of valency information. Modification of PROIEL codes for project needs.

Person Months: 17 PMs

WP5 - Statistical Analysis Months 15-18

Diachronic and Contrastive Analysis

Statistical analyses of valency patterns, genealogical aspects, and contact-induced changes.

WP6 - Dissemination Months 19-24

Results Presentation and Future Planning

Final Valency Lexicon creation, workshops, publications, and evaluation for future expansions.
